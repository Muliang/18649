Simulation Analysis

Our theoretical results show a Best Case usage of 105,395 bits/sec and a Worst Case usage of 126,510 bits/sec. Our experimental results show an overall utilization of 54.86%. This number is the same for multiple tests (including proj10acceptance1, proj10acceptance2, and proj10acceptance3). At 200,000 bit/sec, this is a utilization of 109,720 bits/sec, which is within our theoretical range.

Stuff bits can cause the simulation results to differ, but not to go outside of the theoretical bounds, as the bounds take stuff bits into account. However, our boolean payloads store the boolean value in the top-most bit, which means the number of stuff bits should not vary for these messages. This explains the consistency we saw in our utilization. Another factor may be the randomness of the simulation affects which messages are sent first, which may cause jitter in the utilization. Finally, not prioritizing based on deadline can cause the entire analysis to fail, as a message may miss its deadline.
